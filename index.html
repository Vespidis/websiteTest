My Project

Summary

The intention of the project (GlobeTouch) is to provider customers of the Cruising (and travel) industry, an interactive booking resource that provides a more dynamic experience. This user interface will allow guests to have a more kinaesthetic experience when booking their holidays, by allowing them to “interact” with a world globe, which will then give them all the required information whilst booking their holiday.

Motivation

Of the world’s leading cruise lines, including business’ such as Carnival Cruise Line, Holland America, Norwegian Cruise Line, Royal Caribbean, the vast majority of them offer an extremely basic an objectively boring booking experience, a simple search engine which provides a return list of available cruises which can often be hard to grasp for many potential customers (See example links for common cruise searches). In addition to this, varying studies have shown that in general, a more interactive online experience will increase user satisfaction.

Whilst these list styles will appeal to some visual learners, what it fails to do is capture the “experience” factor and enhance the excitement of travelling to a destination that’s often half the world away. What I hope to do with TouchGlobe (my current name for the project) is provide a booking experience that appeals to not just visual learners, but also kinaesthetic and auditory learners too. Whilst maintaining an efficient and streamlined qualifying/booking experience to maximise revenue.

Project Description

GlobeTouch is an interactive world globe, allowing users to control their destination/holiday planning, by putting them through an interactive qualifying process. Essentially balancing the need for user experience, and valuable data gathering for the parent company.

Initially the guest will interact with a world globe, but before they can look at specific cruises they will be asked the following;

  • Where would you like to travel?:
    This will initiate the GlobeTouch globe, and will spin the globe display to their selected region (ie, europe, spain, chile, mexico) if the guest selects an unavailable destionation, GlobeTouch will reference it's library for nearest ports of origin and suggest that instead
  • When would you like to travel?: MM/YYYY
    This will whittle down available options for cruising, so the guest is not overwhelmed, and will prompt GlobeTouch's next phase the planning phase
The planning phase is the "interactive" aspect to the GlobeTouch booking engine, where the GlobeTouch globe will then "intialise" for the guest, and show their selected region, with ports of origin(cruise start) highlighted by green ship icons, and all other ports of call (accessible from one of the parent companies cruises) highlighted in yellow. The guest can click on any highlighted ports for the following information;
  • Available Cruises
    Available Cruises will show all cruises available for the month and year, that originate or travel through the port selected. If the guest selects a port, GlobeTouch will map their cruise(including direction of travel for best cabin selection) to the globe, and highlight the start/finish port in blue. If the guest chooses to book the cruise at this point, there is a "Book Holiday" button at the top, which will take them through the rest of the booking process.
  • Port Overview
    If the guest selects port information, a summary page is brought up, in-window, which provides a simple description of the port, weather at the time they have selected, port access information (ie transfers etc) and port address/websites
  • Sightseeing / Shore Excursions
    If the guest selects sightseeing/shore excursions they are taken to a zoomed in map of the port, which has title summaries of the excursion available over their location (ie collosseum). If the information is too crowded, a list view can be brought, which again pops up in-window.
